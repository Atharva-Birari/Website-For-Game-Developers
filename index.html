Co-Op Horror with Proximity Voice Chat

Extreme Hype

Games like Lethal Company and Content Warning are dominating. Players enter atmospheric, retro-styled environments to gather loot, run from scary monsters, and experience funny voice communication moments.

How to Capitalize as a Beginner

Since you already published a horror game, build on it! Create a local single-player or simple atmospheric escape horror game. Focus on 3D spatial audio, dark lighting, and a retro/pixelated visual aesthetic to save asset-making time.

02

Liminal Space & Backrooms Explorations

High Virality
Genre: Walking Simulator / Atmospheric Complexity: Low

Uncanny, empty spaces with fluorescent lights and repeating patterns. Gamers love these because they rely heavily on tension, sound design, and VHS camera effects rather than complicated scripting or heavy action systems.

How to Capitalize as a Beginner

This is perfect for Unity. You only need modular corridor assets, yellow wall textures, fluorescent hum audio, and post-processing (Vignette, Film Grain, chromatic aberration) to get that signature camera style.

03

Short, Funny Physics Sandbox Games

Steady Stream
Genre: Physics / Comedy Complexity: Low-Medium

Clumsy controls, chaotic Ragdoll physics, and funny objectives. Gamers play these because they create funny fail moments that are extremely clip-able on short-form platforms (TikTok, YouTube Shorts).

How to Capitalize as a Beginner

Leverage Unity's built-in 3D Physics engine. Build simple levels around a single silly mechanic (e.g., throwing sticky objects, controlling a character with flopping joints) and let the physics engine create the fun.

Direct Installs

Engineering + Game Dev

Entering your first year of engineering is a huge asset. Your courses in math, logic, and programming directly translate to clean code.

  • Version Control (Git): Learn Git early. It prevents project loss and is a core industry tool.
  • Object-Oriented Coding: C# in Unity is excellent practice for your programming courses.
  • Vector Math: Trigonometry and matrices will make coordinate transformations easy.

Beginner Jam Strategy (How to Survive & Win)

As a beginner engineering student, your goal is to finish and submit a working build. Follow these rules:

  • Design around the Theme: Voters judge heavily on how well you implement the jam's constraint. Keep it simple and creative.
  • Scale Down: Take whatever game idea you have, cut 70% of it, and build the remaining 30%. You only need one solid mechanic.
  • Test Early: Build an executable on day one. Make sure it runs. Unity builds can sometimes fail or compile with errors; don't leave compiling to the last 2 hours.
  • Web Builds: If possible, upload a WebGL version to itch.io. People are 10x more likely to play and rate your game if they don't have to download an installer.
